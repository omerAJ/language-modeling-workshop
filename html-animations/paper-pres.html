Thesis Defense

Distribution-Guided and Constrained
Quantum Machine Unlearning

Core claim: data-driven forget targets plus anchor-based constraints can suppress a target class in a variational quantum classifier while retaining useful behavior on the remaining classes.

Thesis scope
Class-level QMU

Constrained optimization instead of full retraining.

Datasets
Iris + Covertype

Low-complexity and higher-complexity settings.

Headline outcome
Strong forgetting

Forgotten-class recall sharply reduced with limited retained-class damage.

Presenter: Nausherwan Malik

Problem

What this thesis is solving

Goal of unlearning

Remove influence of a forgotten subset \(D_f\) from a trained model without retraining from scratch.

\[ U(D, D_f, A(D)) \approx A(D \setminus D_f) \]

In this thesis: targeted class unlearning for quantum classifiers.

Gap in prior quantum unlearning

  • Most existing evaluations are on toy datasets.
  • Unlearning updates are often unconstrained and can drift randomly.
  • Forgetting can stay weak or damage retained behavior.

Method

Distribution-guided + constrained unlearning

The method combines a distribution-guided target for forgotten-class samples with explicit preservation constraints on retained anchors.

Step 1
Train baseline VQC to obtain \(w_{\text{orig}}\) and predictive distributions \(p_{w_{\text{orig}}}(y\mid x)\).
Step 2
Split data into forget set \(F\) (class \(f\)) and anchor set \(A\) (retained classes).
Step 3
Build target \(q\) with \(q_f=0\), based on distribution statistics from \(F\).
Step 4
Optimize unlearning objective with forget term, anchor-preservation term, and parameter anchoring.
Step 5
Evaluate forgetting and compare against a gold retrained model.
Contribution 1: Target design matters Contribution 2: Lagrangian interpretation Contribution 3: Empirical validation on 2 datasets

Objective

Optimization formulation

Distribution-guided forget target

\[ q_k \propto \Big(\mathbb{E}_{x \in S}\; p_{w_{\text{orig}}}(k\mid x)\Big)^{\beta},\quad k \neq f,\quad q_f=0 \]

\(\beta\) controls how sharply mass is concentrated toward the most similar retained class.

Unlearning objective

\[ J(w)=\mathbb{E}_{x\in F}\!\left[\sum_k q_k \log p_w(k\mid x)\right] +\alpha \mathbb{E}_{x\in A}\!\left[\sum_k p_{w_{\text{orig}}}(k\mid x)\log p_w(k\mid x)\right] -\lambda\lVert w-w_{\text{orig}}\rVert_2^2 \]

Interpretation: maximize forgetting while constraining divergence from retained behavior and original parameters.

Theorem 1: this objective is equivalent (up to constants) to a Lagrangian relaxation of constraints on anchor KL and parameter drift.

Setup

Experimental configuration

Model

  • 6-qubit variational quantum classifier
  • Data reuploading + hardware-efficient ansatz
  • 3 repetitions of ring CNOT entanglement
  • 72 trainable parameters

Data

  • Iris (3-class, 4 features)
  • Covertype classes 3/5/7 remapped to 0/1/2
  • Covertype reduced to 4 features via PCA
  • Inputs scaled to \([0,\pi]\)

Training and unlearning

  • Parameter-shift gradients
  • Adam + cosine schedule
  • 300 iterations, batch size 10
  • Default unlearning: \(\alpha=1.0,\lambda=0.01,\beta=1.0\)
Noiseless statevector simulator (Qiskit) Focus is algorithmic behavior, not hardware noise

Ablations

Sensitivity and tuning behavior (Covertype)

Distribution sharpness \(\beta\)

For \(\beta \in \{0.25,0.5,0.75,1.0\}\), accuracy stays in 0.400-0.422 and \(p_f\) in 0.269-0.261.

Interpretation: robust to moderate \(\beta\) changes.

Anchor set size

Anchor fraction 10% -> 25% strongly improves retention; then performance saturates.

A modest anchor subset is often sufficient.

Anchor weight \(\alpha\)

\(\alpha=0\): strongest forgetting (\(p_f=0.214\)) but weak retention (acc 0.300). \(\alpha=1\): balanced (acc 0.411, \(p_f=0.261\)).

Parameter anchoring \(\lambda\)

\(\lambda=0.1\) improves retention (acc 0.433) but weakens forgetting (\(p_f=0.334\)).

Consistent with the Lagrangian trade-off.

Takeaways

What this thesis establishes

Main conclusions

  • Target design is central: distribution-guided targets improve forgetting quality over uniform targets.
  • Anchor-based constraints preserve retained behavior and provide explicit control knobs \((\alpha,\lambda)\).
  • Unlearned models can stay close to gold retraining behavior on retained classes (low KL).

Scope and next steps

  • Experiments are on noiseless simulation, not hardware-noisy execution.
  • Primary evaluation is class-level unlearning in a 3-class setting.
  • Future directions: information-theoretic perspectives for quantum unlearning.
Bottom line: controlled and interpretable QMU is feasible for variational classifiers.
